---
title: "Restrict Test Access by Email Address"
description: "Limit who can take your test by restricting access to specific email domains or individual email addresses using Login Restrictions."
---

You can control which candidates are allowed to take your test by restricting access based on email criteria. This prevents unauthorized users from accessing the test even if they have the test link.

## How to Set Up Email Restrictions

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your test settings">
    Navigate to the test on your AutoProctor dashboard and open its **Test Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Advanced Settings">
    Scroll down to the **Advanced Settings** section and locate the **Login Restrictions** fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add domain or email restrictions">
    Enter the email domains or specific email addresses you want to allow. You can combine both types of restrictions (see examples below).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the test">
    Click **Create** or **Update** to apply the restrictions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Domain-Based Restrictions

Restrict access to candidates whose email addresses end with specific domains. For example, entering `@abc.com` and `@xyz.com` allows only candidates with those email domains to take the test.

</Frame>

## Specific Email Restrictions

You can also permit individual email addresses alongside domain restrictions. This is useful when most candidates share a domain but a few external participants need access.

For example, you can allow all users whose email ends with `@abc.com` plus specific individual email addresses like `guest@gmail.com`.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/results/login-restrictions-email.png" alt="Login Restrictions field showing a combination of domain and individual email restrictions" />
</Frame>

<Note>
Email restrictions work in conjunction with the candidate's [login method](/results/candidate-login-methods). The candidate must sign in with an email address that matches one of the allowed domains or addresses.
</Note>
