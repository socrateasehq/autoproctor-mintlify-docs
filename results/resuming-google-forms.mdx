---
title: "Resuming Google Forms Tests"
description: "Configure whether candidates can resume Google Forms tests after closing them mid-attempt by managing autosave and auto-resume settings."
---

Google Forms tests require two settings to be aligned for resume behavior to work correctly: the **autosave** setting in Google Forms and the **auto-resume** setting in AutoProctor. This guide explains how to configure both.

<Note>
Two settings control Google Forms test resumption:
1. **Disable Autosave** in Google Forms -- Controls whether draft responses are saved as the candidate fills out the form.
2. **Enable Auto-resume** in AutoProctor -- Controls whether AutoProctor loads the previous attempt when a candidate returns to the test link.

Both settings must be configured together for the intended behavior.
</Note>

## To Allow Test Resumption

If you want candidates to pick up where they left off after closing the test:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Keep autosave enabled in Google Forms">
    Make sure the **Disable Autosave** feature is **OFF** in your Google Forms test settings (this is the default).

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/results/gforms-autosave-enabled.png" alt="Google Forms test settings showing Disable Autosave toggled OFF, keeping autosave active" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable auto-resume in AutoProctor">
    Turn on the **Enable Auto-resume** setting in your AutoProctor test settings.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/results/gforms-autoresume-enabled.png" alt="AutoProctor test settings showing Enable Auto-resume toggled ON" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## To Prevent Test Resumption

If you want each visit to the test link to create a new, blank attempt:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Disable autosave in Google Forms">
    Turn **ON** the **Disable Autosave** feature in your Google Forms test settings.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/results/gforms-autosave-disabled.png" alt="Google Forms test settings showing Disable Autosave toggled ON, disabling autosave" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Disable auto-resume in AutoProctor">
    Turn **OFF** the **Resume Unsubmitted Test** option in your AutoProctor test settings.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/results/gforms-autoresume-disabled.png" alt="AutoProctor test settings showing Resume Unsubmitted Test toggled OFF" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
If the two settings are mismatched (for example, autosave is enabled but auto-resume is disabled), candidates may experience unexpected behavior such as seeing a partially filled form on a new attempt or losing their saved progress.
</Warning>
