<Warning>
Please read this page carefully. Improper use of AutoProctor can result in students being unable to take tests or losing their submitted answers.
</Warning>

Hundreds of thousands of students have used AutoProctor successfully, but common mistakes lead to either test access failures or lost submissions. Follow these five requirements to avoid issues.

## 1. No file upload questions

File uploads are not compatible with AutoProctor. Do not include file upload question types in your Google Forms. All other question formats are supported.

## 2. Share instructions with students

Almost all the issues students face can be avoided if they read and follow instructions. Share the appropriate instructions page with your students based on your test configuration:

- For tests with only a timer, share the [timed test instructions](/taking-tests/timed-test-instructions)
- For tests with proctoring, share the [proctored test instructions](/taking-tests/proctored-test-instructions)

<Note>
Have your students complete a demo test at least **24 hours** before their actual exam. This helps them understand the platform and verifies their device is compatible.
</Note>

## 3. Click both submit buttons

<Warning>
If students do not click the purple **Submit** button in AutoProctor, **all their answers will be lost**. This cannot be recovered.
</Warning>

Students must click both submit buttons -- first the one within the test form, and then the purple submit button within AutoProctor. See the [submit button guide](/taking-tests/submit-button) for details.

## 4. Log out of other Google accounts

Students experiencing blank pages, connection errors, or inability to select answers should log out of all their other Google accounts before attempting the test. This resolves the majority of technical issues.

## 5. Maintain sufficient test attempts

AutoProctor requires paid credits. You must ensure you have enough test attempts available in your account, or students will be unable to load the test.
