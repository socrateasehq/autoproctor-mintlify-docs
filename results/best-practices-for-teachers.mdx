title: "Best Practices for Teachers"
description: "Guidance for educators implementing AutoProctor's proctoring feature to ensure successful test administration."
---

Follow these best practices to ensure a smooth testing experience for both you and your students when using AutoProctor's proctoring feature.

## Have students complete a demo test

You absolutely must have students complete a demo test before the actual exam. The demo test helps students understand the platform and confirms their device meets technical requirements.

Use this demo link: [https://www.autoproctor.co/tests/start-test/GFICZZZA](https://www.autoproctor.co/tests/start-test/GFICZZZA)

<Note>
The demo test does not consume test attempts from your account. Share it with students at least **24 hours** before the actual test.
</Note>

## Configure test timing with buffer

When scheduling timed tests with both a duration and a time window, allow extra buffer time. For example, if your test runs for 15 minutes with a scheduled window, allocate 30 minutes total between the start and end times to account for technical delays and student setup.

## Share links properly

- Use **Gmail** or **Google Classroom** to distribute test links rather than in-app browsers (such as Telegram or Facebook), which may cause compatibility issues.
- Advise students to open links directly in **Chrome** or **Firefox** if they receive them through messaging apps with built-in browsers.
- Distribute the demo link at least 24 hours before the actual test.

## Be available during the test

Remain available via a chat application (such as Google Meet or WhatsApp) during testing so students can reach you if they encounter technical issues.

## Clarify results and grading

The AutoProctor tool provides **Trust Scores only** -- actual grades and points appear in your Google Form responses (or your respective quiz platform). Make sure students understand that:

- Technical issues should be directed to AutoProctor support
- Academic questions and grade inquiries should be directed to you, the instructor
