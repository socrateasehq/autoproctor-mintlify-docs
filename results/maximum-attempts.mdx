The **Max Attempts** setting lets you control how many times each candidate can take a specific test. Once a candidate reaches the limit, AutoProctor blocks further attempts and displays a notification.

## How to Configure Maximum Attempts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open test settings">
    Navigate to your test on the AutoProctor dashboard and open its **Test Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the Max Attempts value">
    In the **Main Settings** section, locate the **Max Attempts** field and enter the number of allowed attempts.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/results/max-attempts-setting.png" alt="Max Attempts setting field in AutoProctor Main Settings" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the test">
    Click **Create** or **Update** to apply the setting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

When a candidate exceeds their allowed attempts, they see a restriction message and cannot proceed.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/results/max-attempts-blocked.png" alt="Message shown to candidates when they have exceeded the maximum number of test attempts" />
</Frame>

## Recommended Configuration

<Note>
For Google Forms and Socratease Quizzes, set **Max Attempts** to **1**. AutoProctor automatically [resumes these tests](/results/resuming-test-attempts) if candidates reload the page or revisit the link, so multiple attempts are not needed for resumption.
</Note>

## How Attempts Are Tracked

AutoProctor tracks attempts by the email address used to sign in. This means:

- Each unique email address is treated as a separate candidate, regardless of who is behind it.
- If a candidate signs in with different email addresses across attempts (for example, using **Sign in with Google** once and **Sign in with Email** another time), each email counts separately, effectively bypassing the attempt limit.

## Preventing Attempt Limit Bypass

To prevent candidates from circumventing the maximum attempts setting:

- **Restrict login methods** -- Choose a test type that supports only one sign-in option (Google Forms restricts to Google only, Microsoft Forms restricts to Microsoft only). See [Candidate Login Methods](/results/candidate-login-methods).
- **Use email restrictions** -- Limit access to specific email domains or addresses so candidates cannot use alternative accounts. See [Restrict Test Access by Email Address](/results/restricting-by-email).
