title: "Track Your Test Pack Credit Usage"
description: "Monitor how your credits are being consumed across tests and team members using the Usage dashboard in your AutoProctor account."
---

AutoProctor provides a dedicated Usage dashboard that shows exactly how your credits are being consumed. You can track usage per test and, if you are part of a team, per team member.

## Viewing Your Credit Usage

Credits are added to your account when you create a subscription, when it renews, or when you purchase Test Pack top-ups. Each time a candidate starts a test, one credit is deducted.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Usage page">
    Click on the **Usage** tab in your left sidebar.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/pricing/usage-sidebar-link.png" alt="Usage link highlighted in the left sidebar navigation" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the usage table">
    You will see a table showing the number of credits used per test. Click on any test title to open the results for all attempts of that test.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/pricing/usage-table.png" alt="Usage table displaying credits consumed per test" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
If a test has been shared with multiple test administrators, only the **primary admin (the test creator)** will see it listed on their Usage page.
</Note>

## Team Usage

If you are part of a [Team](/pricing/teams), the Usage page shows your personal test data by default. To view credit consumption across the entire team, click the **Team Usage** button at the top right of the page.

The team usage view displays a table with **all team members and the total number of credits each member has used**. You can click on any member to drill down into their individual test-level usage.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/pricing/team-usage-table.png" alt="Team usage table showing credit consumption per team member" />
</Frame>

<Note>
Only Team Admins and the member themselves can view detailed per-test usage. Regular team members can see the summary view but not other members' individual breakdowns.
</Note>
