## Cancelling Your Subscription

Cancelling your AutoProctor subscription is very simple -- just 3 clicks.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Billing section">
    From your dashboard, click on the **Billing** link on the left sidebar.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/pricing/billing-sidebar-link.png" alt="Billing link in sidebar" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Manage Subscription">
    If you have an active subscription, you will see a **Manage Subscription** button in the Plan Details section.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/pricing/manage-subscription-button.png" alt="Manage Subscription button" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cancel the plan">
    Click on the **Cancel plan** button on the right.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/pricing/cancel-plan-button.png" alt="Cancel plan button" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What happens after you click the Cancel plan button?">
    Let's say you had an active monthly subscription that was due to renew on 10th March. You cancelled your subscription on 5th March. Your account will actually **remain active until 10th March** -- the subscription is scheduled to cancel only on 10th March.

    So, you and your candidates can continue to use AutoProctor until 10th March. You can even top up your account until then.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens after the subscription is actually cancelled?">
    Your subscription status changes to **Expired**. With an Expired subscription:

    - Your candidates **cannot attempt new tests**
    - You **cannot see all the results** of existing tests
    - Your credits (test attempt) balance is **reset to 0** (if you have a positive balance)

    Read Qs 11-13 on the [Pricing Page](https://www.autoproctor.co/pricing) for more details.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are my tests retained?">
    Yes. The tests you have created and their settings are **not deleted**. You will not be able to access all the results of the tests, though.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I renew my subscription after it is cancelled?">
    Technically, you cannot renew it -- you will create a new subscription. But for all practical purposes, yes. You can resume using your account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My subscription is scheduled to be cancelled. Can I undo this?">
    Yes. You can follow the same steps listed above to Manage your subscription and undo the scheduled cancellation.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
