title: "Archiving and Deleting Tests"
description: "Learn how to archive tests to declutter your dashboard or permanently delete tests you no longer need."
---

Over time, your dashboard may accumulate demo tests, completed exams, and outdated assessments. Use archiving to hide tests from your main view, or delete them permanently when they are no longer needed.

## Archiving Tests

Archiving a test removes it from your main dashboard without deleting it. This is useful for hiding demo or completed tests while keeping them accessible.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/creating-tests/archive-test.png" alt="Archive Test option in test settings" />
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Test Settings">
    Go to your **Dashboard** and open the test's **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Archive the Test">
    Click **Archive Test** to move the test out of your main dashboard view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access Archived Tests">
    Visit the **Archived Tests** section in the left sidebar to view, restore, or delete archived tests.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Deleting Tests

You can permanently delete tests from the **Archived Tests** section. Only archived tests can be deleted — you must archive a test first.

<Warning>
Deleting a test removes it from your account, but **candidate answers and proctoring records are preserved**. That data persists even after the test is deleted.
</Warning>

### Important Notes

- Deleted tests may reappear if the original quiz provider (such as Google Forms) is accessed again
- In multi-collaborator scenarios, deletion only affects the account that initiated it — other collaborators retain access to the test
