## What are Question Banks?

Question Banks are a large pool of questions. Candidates will see a random subset of the questions, based on the Question Bank chosen and difficulty level. This makes each candidate's test unique, reducing the chance of cheating.

## How Question Banks Work

A Question Bank (QB) is a collection of questions organized by subject or topic. Each question in the bank can have a different point value and difficulty level.

### Example: Physics and Chemistry Tests

Imagine you have two Question Banks:
- **Physics Question Bank** -- 100 questions
- **Chemistry Question Bank** -- 100 questions

You can create different quizzes that draw from these banks with different weightings:

- **Physics Major Quiz**: 40 two-point physics questions + 10 one-point chemistry questions
- **Chemistry Major Quiz**: 40 two-point chemistry questions + 10 one-point physics questions

Each candidate receives a different random subset of questions from the respective banks, but the overall structure (number of questions, points, difficulty distribution) remains consistent.

## Question Bank Quizzes (QBQ)

Your candidates cannot attempt a Question Bank directly. You must create a **Question Bank Quiz (QBQ)** and add it to an AutoProctor test.

A Question Bank Quiz specifies:
- Which Question Banks to draw questions from
- How many questions to include from each bank
- The difficulty levels to include
