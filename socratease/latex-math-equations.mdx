## Why LaTeX?

With platforms like Google Forms and similar tools, if you want to include a mathematical equation in an exam, you must either insert equation images or use simplified text notation (like `a^2` or `3/4`). This is cumbersome and difficult to read.

**LaTeX is the global standard language used to write mathematical equations**, found in academic publications, textbooks, and research papers worldwide.

## How to Use LaTeX

To include a LaTeX equation in your quiz, enclose the equation between `\(` and `\)`.

**Example:**

```
\(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\)
```

This renders as the familiar Pythagorean theorem: a squared plus b squared equals c squared.

## What LaTeX Supports

LaTeX can render any mathematical notation, including:

- **Basic arithmetic**: `\(a + b = c\)`
- **Exponents and subscripts**: `\(x^2\)`, `\(a_n\)`
- **Fractions**: `\(\frac{a}{b}\)`
- **Square roots**: `\(\sqrt{x}\)`
- **Integrals**: `\(\int_0^1 x^2 dx\)`
- **Summations**: `\(\sum_{i=1}^{n} i\)`
- **Greek letters**: `\(\alpha, \beta, \gamma\)`
- And any other advanced mathematical notation

## Enabling LaTeX

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Your Quiz">
    Open your Socratease Quiz on AutoProctor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Settings">
    Click the **Settings** icon.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable LaTeX Support">
    Toggle on the **LaTeX Support** setting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write Equations">
    Write your equations in question text using the `\(` and `\)` delimiters.
  </Step>
</Steps>
