AutoProctor assigns a Trust Score (0–100%) to each proctoring report. The score provides a quick summary of how likely it is that a candidate maintained test integrity.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/getting-started/trustscore.png" alt="Trust Score display in an AutoProctor proctoring report" />
</Frame>

The lower the Trust Score, the more likely AutoProctor detected suspicious behavior during the test.

<Warning>
Always review the supporting evidence before drawing conclusions. **Do not** rely solely on the Trust Score — review the actual violation evidence to determine whether misconduct occurred.
</Warning>

## How the Trust Score Is Calculated

AutoProctor monitors candidates in real-time across multiple channels (depending on your [test settings](/creating-tests/test-settings)):

- Camera feed
- Microphone feed
- Screen activity

The algorithm evaluates violations based on three factors:

1. **Type of violation** — tab switching is weighted more heavily than noise detection
2. **Frequency of violations** — more incidents result in a lower score
3. **Duration of violations** — extended violations have a greater impact than brief ones

## What Is a Good Trust Score?

As a general guideline, review the evidence for any candidate with a Trust Score **below 85%**. This threshold is meant for review, not as proof of misconduct.

<Note>
Environmental factors can significantly impact scoring. For example, ambient noise from traffic could lower a score to 0% even when no actual cheating occurred.
</Note>
