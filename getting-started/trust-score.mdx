AutoProctor displays a Trust Score (0–100%) on each proctoring report.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/getting-started/trustscore.png" alt="Trust Score display in AutoProctor report" />
</Frame>

The lower the trust score, the more likely AutoProctor thinks the candidate cheated on the test.

<Warning>
You **must** review the supporting evidence before making any conclusions. **DO NOT** rely solely on the Trust Score — always review the actual evidence to determine if cheating occurred.
</Warning>

## How Is the Trust Score Calculated?

AutoProctor monitors candidates in real-time across multiple channels (depending on your settings):

- Camera feed
- Microphone feed
- Screen activity

The algorithm evaluates violations based on three factors:

1. **Type of violation** — tab switching is weighted more heavily than noise detection
2. **Frequency of violations** — 10 incidents scores lower than 5
3. **Duration of violations** — extended violations have a greater impact

## What Is a Good Trust Score?

As a general guideline, if the Trust Score is **lower than 85%**, you may want to review the evidence. However, this is a threshold for review, not proof of misconduct.

<Note>
Environmental factors significantly impact scoring. For example, ambient noise from traffic could lower scores to 0% despite no actual cheating occurring.
</Note>
