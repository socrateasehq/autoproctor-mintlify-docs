---
title: "Missing Images and Recordings"
description: "Understand why images or audio recordings may not appear in a student's test report despite violations being listed."
---

If you can see violations listed in a student's test report but cannot see the supporting evidence (images or audio recordings), there are two possible scenarios.

## Scenario 1: No images or recordings at all

If no images or recordings appear at all despite violations being listed, the "Me after the test" recording feature was likely disabled in the test settings.

<Note>
Check your test's proctoring settings to ensure evidence recording is enabled. See [AutoProctor Test Settings](/creating-tests/test-settings) for more information about the different settings and what they mean.
</Note>

## Scenario 2: Some violations have evidence, others don't

If some violations show evidence files while others don't, this is expected behavior. AutoProctor stores a fixed number (around 20) of images and audio files per test attempt.

<Warning>
**ALL violations are tracked and affect the Trust Score**, even when evidence files are not stored for viewing. The evidence files are a subset -- they exist so that administrators can review what happened, but the Trust Score calculation considers every detected violation regardless of whether evidence was stored.
</Warning>
